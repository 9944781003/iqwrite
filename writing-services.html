Research articles are intended to reveal a scholar’s academic knowledge of a subject. A proposal is a persuasive piece meant to convince its audience of the value of a research project. Think of the proposal as the pitch and the article as the finished product.


Sample Areas For Writing Research Papers

Choose your topic carefully; make sure it is something that interests you.
Use all the resources your library offers: journals, books, digital platforms, etc.

Color code your research notes by theme. It will make it easier when you structure and write your paper.

Make sure you keep bibliographic data on all your research notes so you can be sure your referencing is precise.

Make a thesis statement that is clear, arguable and precise.
Plan your article before you start writing. A well thought out outline will offer direction to your writing and guarantee you stay on topic.

When you’ve finished writing, read it through and ask whether you have expressed what you set out to say.

Carefully proofread your last draft, check for grammatical and typographical errors, and accurately reference every source.
